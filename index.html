Research

I earned my PhD in Biomedical Informatics in 2023 advised by Peter Park at HMS . For my dissertation, I studied the origins, patterns, and consequences of somatic mutations in cancer, brain development , and gene editing. I was an NIH/NCI F31 NRSA Predoctoral Fellow during my PhD (2021-2023).

As a postdoctoral fellow (since June 2023) in the Park Lab, I continue to study somatic mutations in these three settings by developing computational methods to detect somatic mutations from emerging technologies.

Before my PhD, I was a master's student in immunology (2015-2016) and later a technician (2016-2017) in the laboratory of Shiv Pillai at the Ragon Institute of MGH, MIT, and Harvard . I I studied DNA methylation dynamics in B-cell differentiation . I also helped identify a spontaneous copy-number variant in C56BL/6 and studied its consequences .

Before my maaster's degree, I earned my undergraduate degrees in Bioengineering and Molecular and Cell Biology from UC Berkeley (2010-2014).
